Experiences

Through my time at Georgia Tech, I have garnered an interest in design. Through my newfound interest, I have taken steps to learn more and gain experience in the field.

I recently learned to create image layouts for magazines using other students' works of art. I have also had to chance to design websites and other branding images, as well as learning about the design and web development process.

Though I still have a long way to go, I have enjoyed what I have worked on so far and I hope to use these skills in the future to create better and improved works.

My First Wireframing

In this project, I was tasked by a web developer at HexLabs to create a design for participants at the annual hackathon, HackGT, to redeem points from mini events to receive prizes. I had to find a way to incorporate all the components and functionalities the developers aimed to have on this portal. I actually realized how much I enjoyed wireframing at this part, because it felt like a puzzle. How could I make this site look nice and perform all the desired functions? We had the theme of a night market, so inspired by the dark blues of the night and the lights and scenery of the outdoor market, I created a high fidelity wireframe of what I wanted this to look like. Granted, it was not as perfect as I had hoped, nor did I even get all the information to make it a truly high fidelity product, but it was a great experience and I definitely improved since then.

Current Work

Right now, I am currently working on a case study on a possible travel app for Kyoto, Japan. During this time, I have learnt valuable UX research skills, as well as design tips. I also did not work on this project alone, but for this sake, I have compiled all of my work onto a file alone. Lots of iterations of this design has been made, as we discussed it, we realized that there were a lot of unecessary factors which caused us to change our design a lot. For example, we deleted the "My Trips" section because it was very redundant with the diary section. We have still not finalized our high fidelity yet. Compared to the project above, I really think that my skills have grown a lot since then. I have gotten a lot more comfortable with Figma, as seen in my wireframes. I really enjoy working on this project because it has been really interesting and a great learning experience. I am excited to see the final product!
